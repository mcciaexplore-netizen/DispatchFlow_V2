1. What is DispatchFlow?

Understanding what this app does in everyday language

Every day your factory sends out goods. Each shipment has a paper slip — the customer's name, what was sent, how many pieces, the vehicle number. At the end of the day, someone has to sit down and type all of this into a register or Excel file. This takes a long time and mistakes happen easily.

DispatchFlow solves this problem. You simply take a photo of the paper slip using a camera, and the app reads all the information automatically — just like how you scan a QR code on your phone. It fills in the details for you. You check it, click Save, and you are done. The whole thing takes about 2 minutes instead of 15.

❌ The Old Way

  • Type everything by hand into Excel
  • Spelling mistakes and wrong numbers
  • Hard to find a record from 3 months ago
  • Paper slips get lost or torn
  • 1–2 hours of data entry every day
  • No copy kept anywhere safe

✅ With DispatchFlow

  • Take a photo — app fills the form
  • Data is always correct
  • Search any record in 5 seconds
  • Everything stored safely on your device
  • Under 2 minutes per slip
  • Automatic backup to Google Sheets
💡 Think of it like this DispatchFlow is like having a very fast and accurate office assistant who reads your paper slips, types the data for you, stores it safely, and can find any record whenever you need it — all for free.

3. How Does It Work?

A simple explanation — no technical words used

You do not need to understand the technology. Here is the simple story of what happens when you scan a dispatch slip:

  • 1
    You hold the paper slip under the camera

    A live picture of the slip appears on your screen — just like using the camera on your phone.

  • 2
    You press the Capture button

    The app takes a photo of the slip. A small loading bar appears for a few seconds.

  • 3
    The app reads all the text on the slip

    A program called OCR looks at the photo and reads every word and number on it — like how your eye reads a page, but much faster. It understands Hindi, Marathi, and English.

  • 4
    Google's AI fills in the form for you

    A smart program understands which piece of text belongs where — customer name goes in the customer field, quantity goes in the quantity field, and so on. Fields it has filled are shown highlighted in orange so you can easily spot them.

  • 5
    You check the form and click Save

    Look at the form, fix anything if needed, and click Save. Done. The record is now safely stored.

  • 6
    The record appears in your Google Sheet automatically

    If you have connected Google Sheets, the entry is added there too — in the background, without you doing anything extra.

6. One-Time Setup

This section is for your IT person or computer-savvy staff member

⚠️ For Business Owners — Read This First This section describes the technical setup that only needs to happen once, and only needs to be done by your IT person or someone comfortable with computers. After this is done, everyone else just opens the app in their browser — no technical steps needed.

If you are the business owner reading this — hand this section to your IT support person and ask them to complete the setup. Then skip to Section 7.

What the IT Person Needs to Install

The app has two parts that need to be installed on the computer once:

A

The Reading Engine

This is the behind-the-scenes program that looks at photos of slips and reads the text. It runs quietly in the background. Your IT person installs this using the setup files in the ocr-backend folder. Full technical instructions are in the README.md file included with the application.

B

The Web App (What You See on Screen)

This is the screens, buttons, and forms you use daily. Your IT person sets this up from the ocr-frontend folder. After setup, you open it by visiting a simple address in your web browser — like opening a website. Full instructions are in the README.md file.

✅ After the Setup is Done The IT person should create a simple shortcut on the Desktop so that every morning, staff just double-click it and the app is ready. They open their browser, go to the app address, and start working — no commands, no technical steps.
ℹ️ How Long Does Setup Take? About 30–45 minutes total for a first-time setup, mostly because the reading engine downloads some files automatically during first start. After that, startup every day takes about 30 seconds.

10. Scanning a Dispatch Slip

The main daily task — from paper to digital in under 2 minutes

Using the Camera

1

Click "New Dispatch" on the home screen

A large orange button on the home screen. The scan page will open.

2

Click the camera button to turn on the camera

Your browser may ask for camera permission the first time — click Allow. A live video of what the camera sees will appear on the left side of the screen.

3

Hold the slip flat under the camera and click Capture

The whole slip should be visible. Make sure there is good light. Avoid shadows falling on the slip. Hold the camera directly above, not at an angle.

4

Wait 5–15 seconds for the form to fill itself

A loading bar appears. When it finishes, all the fields on the right side of the screen will be filled automatically. Orange/amber coloured fields were filled by the app — these are the ones to quickly check.

5

Check the form and correct anything if needed

Click on any field that looks wrong and type the correct value. This takes about 20–30 seconds.

6

Click "Save" or "Save & Print"

The record is now saved. If you chose "Save & Print", the print screen opens immediately.

Using a Photo Instead of the Camera

If you already have a photo of the slip (taken on your phone and transferred to the computer, or received on WhatsApp), you can upload it instead:

  • Click the Upload button on the scan screen
  • Choose the photo file from your computer
  • The rest works the same — the app reads and fills the form automatically
✅ Tips for Getting the Best Results from the Camera
  • Use a well-lit area — a desk near a window works well
  • Lay the slip flat on a plain surface (white table or plain desk)
  • Hold the camera or position the webcam directly above — not from the side
  • Make sure the full slip is visible, with no edges cut off
  • Avoid shiny paper — if the slip is glossy, tilt it very slightly to remove the glare

14. Taking a Backup

Protect your data — a weekly backup takes 10 seconds

⚠️ Very Important — Please Read Your records are stored inside the web browser on your computer. If someone clears the browser data, reinstalls the computer, or the browser resets — all records can be lost.

Take a backup at least once a week. It takes 10 seconds and can save months of data.

How to Take a Backup

  1. Click the Settings icon in the top-right corner
  2. Enter your Admin PIN when asked
  3. Scroll to the section called "Data Management"
  4. Click the button "Export Backup Now"
  5. A file will be downloaded to your computer with today's date in its name
  6. Copy this file to a USB drive, or email it to yourself as an attachment

How to Restore from a Backup

  1. Go to Settings → Data Management
  2. Click "Import from Backup"
  3. Select the backup file from your computer or USB drive
  4. All your records will be restored. Nothing will be overwritten or deleted.
✅ The App Reminds You Automatically Every Monday morning, a reminder banner appears on the home screen: "Time to take your weekly backup!" Just click the button on the banner and save the file. Do not click "Later" and ignore it — your data is important!

The backup reminder banner — click "Download Backup" as soon as you see this

16. Managing Your Staff on the App

Track which staff member created each record — no separate logins needed

Every time someone opens DispatchFlow, the app asks for their name. This name — called the operator name — is stored with every record they create. This way, you always know who made each entry.

How It Works Day-to-Day

  • When a staff member starts their shift, they open the app and type their name
  • Previous names appear as quick-tap buttons — no need to retype every day
  • All records they create that session will show their name
  • When a different person sits down, they click the name shown in the top bar and enter their own name

Using It for Accountability

In the History page, you can filter records by operator name. This lets you see:

  • How many slips each staff member entered today or this week
  • Which records a specific person created
  • If any records are missing (were not entered)

The name prompt — tap a previous name to select it instantly, or type a new one

ℹ️ No Separate Passwords for Staff Staff members do not need separate passwords or accounts. They just type their name. Only the Settings page needs a PIN (the Admin PIN that only the owner knows).

17. Keeping the App Safe

The PIN system and auto-lock features explained simply

Admin PIN

The Settings page is protected by a 4–8 digit PIN that you chose during setup. Without this PIN, no one can change settings, delete records, or export backups. Only the business owner and trusted senior management should know this PIN.

App Auto-Lock

You can set the app to automatically lock after a period of no activity — for example, if a staff member walks away from the computer and leaves it open. When locked, the next person must enter the operator name to continue. This does NOT require the Admin PIN.

19. Common Questions

Things business owners and staff often ask

Do I need internet to use the app every day?
No. After setup, the app works completely without internet. If you have Google Sheets connected, it will sync the new records to your sheet as soon as internet is available. But data entry, scanning, and searching all work without any internet connection.
What if the computer is reset or replaced?
If the computer is reset without a backup, the records stored inside the browser will be lost. This is why weekly backups are so important (Section 14). With a recent backup file, you can restore all records on any new computer within minutes. If you have Google Sheets sync enabled, your data is also safely stored there.
Can I use this on my phone or tablet?
Yes. Open the app address in Google Chrome on your phone. Everything works the same — including the camera scanning using your phone's rear camera. The design is made for touch screens with large, easy-to-tap buttons.
What if the app fills in wrong information from the slip?
Simply click on that field and type the correct value. The orange-highlighted fields show you which ones the app filled — those are the ones to check. For the best accuracy, make sure the slip is well-lit, flat, and the camera is directly above (not at an angle).
Can multiple staff members use the same computer?
Yes. Each person just enters their name at the start of their session. All records from all staff are stored together and searchable. You can filter by name to see one person's records.
Is there any monthly charge to use DispatchFlow?
No. DispatchFlow itself is free. The Google Gemini AI key (which reads your slips) has a free usage limit that is more than enough for most small businesses. Google Sheets is also free. The only cost is your internet connection and the computer you already have.
Can I use it for invoices as well as dispatch slips?
Yes. DispatchFlow handles both. The invoice screen even handles multi-item invoices — it reads every product line on the invoice and extracts them all separately. Invoices and dispatch slips are stored and searchable separately.
What languages does it read?
DispatchFlow reads English, Hindi (Devanagari script), and Marathi. It can handle slips that mix languages — very common in Indian factory environments. Even handwritten text added in Hindi on a printed slip will be read correctly.
I forgot my Admin PIN. What do I do?
Contact your IT person. Resetting the PIN requires clearing the app's stored data — which will also delete all records. This is why keeping a weekly backup is so important. With a backup, you can reset the app and restore all your records immediately.

21. Simple Explanations of App Words

Every unfamiliar word or phrase explained in plain language

Word You See in the AppWhat It Actually Means
OCR The technology that reads text from a photo — like how your eye reads a printed page, but done by the computer. Stands for Optical Character Recognition — but you never need to remember that.
AI / Gemini Google's artificial intelligence. It looks at the text read from your slip and figures out which word goes in which field — like a very fast and clever assistant.
Operator The person using the app. Just your name — so records show who created each entry.
Admin PIN A 4–8 digit number that protects the Settings area. Like the password to a safe — only the owner should know it.
Sync Sending your records automatically to Google Sheets in the background. The green/amber/red dot in the top bar shows if this is working.
Backup A copy of all your records saved as a single file. Like a photocopy of your entire register that you keep in a different place, in case the original is damaged.
Restore Loading records back from a backup file. Like opening that photocopy and putting the information back.
Grace Period / Edit Window The time window after saving a record when you can still change it. After this window closes, the record is locked and cannot be changed.
Schema / Template The list of fields on your form. Choosing a template is like picking a pre-printed form that already has the right columns for your business.
API Key A secret code from Google that gives DispatchFlow permission to use Google's AI. Like a key to a room — once you have it and put it in, the door stays open.
CSV / Export Downloading all your records as a file that opens in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets. Useful for monthly reports or sharing with your accountant.
Sync Log A history of every time the app tried to send records to Google Sheets — showing which ones succeeded and which failed. Like a delivery receipt book.
